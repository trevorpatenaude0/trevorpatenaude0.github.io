AI in Medical Diagnostics

Context: Research paper written for Cooperative Education for Technology, Winter 2026. I chose this topic because I'm interested in how technology can make healthcare more accurate and efficent.

The topic I have decided to look into was the AI and Machine Learning in Diagnostics, the reason I picked this topic is because to me it seemed the most impactful out of all the options. Having AI within the hospitals themselves could potentially be the next wave of technology we see coming to society, but how reliable and accurate is it really? I want to explore and develop ideas around how and why this is a good thing while also waging the other side of the argument as well.

When first looking into this idea my first thoughts were how much integrity this really has, can AI really be better than a licensed doctor and hold its own against one. But the more I read and went deeper into the articles that’s not what the main point was at all. The AI purpose was not being used as a replacement to the doctors but rather a tool for them. The AI was allowing for doctors to implement fast advance technologies, some examples of this are tracking mechanism linked to people's vitals, consulting support, and even things like imaging analysis. But the idea isn’t for the AI to tell the doctor what’s right or wrong, the idea is for the doctors to use fast, real time AI, to help solidify their ideas and take the appropriate measures to help. The other big integrity discussion in this article is what AI they are using and how exactly it formulates an answer. Dr. Leana Wen explained it best in this portion of the article saying “First, it helps to know the difference between predictive and generative artificial intelligence, or AI.” “Predictive AI uses mathematical models and pattern recognition to predict the future. For For example, a predictive AI algorithm can identify which patients with pneumonia are most likely to require hospitalization.” Going off what Dr. Leane Wen said here clearly proves the AI isn’t taking over as the doctor but rather making a mathematical decision based off previously collected data and helped the doctors make a decision.

When it comes to finding out the limitations of these AI models and the integrity they have one of the best ways to test this would be by having it inside of the hospitals and seeing how it holds up. Unfortunately I see this being one of the things that takes a while to develop not because of just the technical complications but rather the willingness of people. Not only would patients have to be okay with submitting their personal data to the learning modules, it would have to be thousands of patients to build a strong enough model to get the widest most accurate range of answers. The other complication I see is also the willingness from hospitals and doctors around the world, as they would now have to implement the modules and make the doctors utilize and document how useful and accurate the AI is. By needing all these expensive implementations and changing the work flow I see a normal everyday hospital never adapting these ideas… unless becoming a social norm, one day in the future.

Another article I read upon this topic explained the imagining portion of the AI and how useful it really is. The article put into realization how useful the imaging portion can be “Take lung nodules, for example, if your doctor spots a small spot on your lung during a scan, they might not know right away if it’s something to worry about. They’ll typically recommend follow-up scans to look for changes.” The AI during this phase would take other renders of people in the same condition and review the images comparing and contrasting potentially alerting the doctor of any changes or things they missed at first. The AI also helps the conversation between the radiologist and doctors between scans, making sure any tiny growths or small variations are not lost in translation between the doctors.

Overall I think that AI could totally change the way hospitals are manages and operated based on time, giving the doctors this tool could not only save thousands of lives but also allow for continued monitoring outside the hospitals. Having the doctors be able to detect issues and give a diagnosis even faster is seemingly possibly with AI and I believe these articles prove that. The way our society is provided and cared for could drastically increase with this implementation for all.
